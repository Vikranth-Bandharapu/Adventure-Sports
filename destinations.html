The Himalayas
The Roof of the World

The Himalayas form the world's highest mountain range, hosting some of the most iconic trekking routes on earth. From the spiritual trails of Kedarnath to the dramatic ridgelines of Roopkund, every path here tells an ancient story.

Kedarkantha & Roopkund — signature WildPeak treks
High-altitude base camp expeditions (4500m+)
Valley of Flowers UNESCO World Heritage trail
Explore Himalayas
Forests
Western Ghats
A UNESCO Biodiversity Hotspot

One of the world's eight "hottest biodiversity hotspots", the Western Ghats host over 5,000 flowering plant species, 139 mammal species and countless endemic birds. Trek through ancient shola forests and spot leopards in their natural habitat.

Night safari in Nagarhole & Bandipur forests
Kumara Parvatha summit trek (1712m)
Athirappilly waterfall trek & river crossing
Explore Western Ghats

Rishikesh
India's Adventure Capital

Nestled where the Ganges descends to the plains, Rishikesh is the undisputed adventure capital of India. White water rafting, bungee jumping from India's highest platform, forest camping and morning yoga on the Ganges banks.

16km–36km grade rafting expeditions on Ganga
Bungee jumping at Mohan Chatti (83m platform)
Himalayan forest camping & bonfire nights
